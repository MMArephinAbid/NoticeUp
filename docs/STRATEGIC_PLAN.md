## FIRST SAAS OPTIONS (Decision Pending)

### Option 1: FundFlow (Donation Platform)

**Pros:**
- Universal (Churches, Mosques, Schools, NGOs)
- Clear path to MFS
- Compliance = moat

**Cons:**
- Competes with FREE (Zeffy, GiveButter)
- Saturated market
- "Nice-to-have" not "must-have"

### Option 2: Membership Hub (Dues Management)

**Pros:**
- High switching cost (data lock-in)
- Recurring revenue x2 (SaaS + transaction)
- Weak competition (Wild Apricot is old/expensive)
- Path to MFS

**Cons:**
- Niche market
- Slower growth

### Option 3: Client Portal (for Agencies)

**Pros:**
- B2B = higher ARPU
- Data lock-in
- Professional market

**Cons:**
- More competition
- Harder to build

### Option 4: Home Services Invoice (Paid Today)

**Pros:**
- Huge market ($500B+)
- Simple to build

**Cons:**
- Can't guarantee payment
- Low switching cost
- Commodity product

---

## Key Requirements for First SaaS

1. **Easy to build** (7-14 days MVP)
2. **Good MRR** ($29-99/month)
3. **High switching cost** (clients won't leave)
4. **Global market** (not country-specific)
5. **Not saturated** (untapped opportunity)
6. **Universal positioning** (not Muslim-centric branding)
7. **Path to ERP + MFS** (aligns with vision)

---

## What Creates High Switching Cost?

| Factor | Why They're Stuck |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Data accumulates** | Years of records, can't export |
| **External users depend on it** | Their customers/members use it |
| **Compliance/Legal records** | Need historical data |
| **Payment history** | Tax, audit trail |
| **Link shared everywhere** | URL is on business cards, website |

---

## Recommendation: Membership Hub

**Why:**

```
1. EASIEST BUILD
   - CRUD for members
   - Stripe for payments
   - Basic dashboard

2. HIGHEST LOCK-IN
   - 500 members' data
   - 3 years payment history
   - Members have portal login

3. RECURRING REVENUE x2
   - SaaS fee ($49/mo)
   - Transaction fee (1%)

4. COMPETITION IS WEAK
   - Wild Apricot: $100+/mo, old UI
   - No modern, affordable option

5. PERFECT PATH TO MFS
   - Start: Collect dues
   - Next: Member loans (Qard Hasan)
   - Future: Full MFS for communities
```
